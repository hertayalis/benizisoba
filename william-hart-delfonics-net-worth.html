William Hart Delfonics Net Worth

In 1965, William Hart worked as a barber in a Philadelphia shop. One day, William Hart, who had already written a number of songs by this point, and Stan Watson walked into the barbershop.

Watson told William Hart about a young arranger/producer named Thom Bell who was then working for Cameo-Parkway Records with Chubby Checker. Bell was then introduced to the group by Watson. William Hart recalls playing Bell his original song “He Don’t Really Love You” for the first time.

Bell created the song’s music right away, and it was released on Moon Shot Records, which later changed its name to Cameo-Parkway Records.

Before forming the Delfonics, William “Poogie” Hart sang in a number of bands, including Little Hart and The Everglows, the Veltones, the Four Guys, and the Four Gents.

Several of these early groups included William and Wilbert Hart, Ritchie Daniels, Randy Cain, Stan Lathan, and Donald Cannon, pals who first met in the 1960s at Overbrook High School.

The Orphonics, which also comprised Richie Daniels and Randy Cain, was created in 1964 by William “Poogie” Hart and his brother Wilbert Hart.
