Chuba Akpom Height: How Tall is Chuba Akpom? – Chuba Akpom is a professional footballer specializing as a striker for the Eredivisie club Ajax.

Born on October 9, 1995, Akpom initiated his professional journey at Arsenal, making his debut in 2013. While with Arsenal, he underwent loan spells with Brentford, Coventry City, Nottingham Forest, Hull City, Brighton, and Belgian club Sint-Truiden. In 2018, he joined Greek club PAOK, and after a successful stint, he moved to Middlesbrough in 2020. In 2021, he returned to PAOK on loan, earning the status of a club icon and playing a pivotal role in their double-winning squad of 2018–19, notably scoring the decisive goal in the 2019 Greek Cup Final.

London-born to Nigerian parents, Akpom holds dual citizenship in the United Kingdom and Nigeria. He initially represented England in youth and under-21 levels but announced his switch to Nigeria’s international allegiance in 2019.

Akpom’s association with Arsenal began at the age of six. He debuted for the Arsenal U18 side at 15 and signed his first professional contract on October 10, 2012. His senior debut for Arsenal came in a 3–1 Premier League win over Sunderland on September 14, 2013. Akpom also contributed to Arsenal’s success in the League Cup, scoring a crucial penalty in a shootout against West Bromwich Albion.

During various loan spells, Akpom played for clubs like Brentford, Coventry City, Nottingham Forest, Hull City, Brighton, and Sint-Truiden. His notable achievements include a hat-trick in a 6–1 FA Youth Cup fourth-round victory over Peterborough United in 2014.

Akpom’s journey continued with Middlesbrough, where he made a significant impact, leading to a contract extension. His notable achievements include a hat-trick against Wigan Athletic and becoming the first Middlesbrough player to score 20 league goals in a season since 1990.

In August 2023, Akpom signed a five-year contract with Dutch Eredivisie club Ajax. He made his debut in a 0–0 draw against Fortuna Sittard on September 3, 2023, and scored his first goal for Ajax in a 2–0 win over Volendam on November 2, 2023.

Akpom’s international career includes representing England at various youth levels and making appearances for the U17s, U19s, U20s, and U21s. Despite being eligible to represent Nigeria, Akpom initially chose to represent England but eventually confirmed his intention to play for Nigeria in September 2019.
