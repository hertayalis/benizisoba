Is Ashanti Expecting First Child With Nelly?

Is Ashanti Expecting First Child With Nelly? – Ashanti is an accomplished American singer and actress. Initially discovered as a teenager, she secured a record deal with Murder Inc. Records in 2002.

Born on October 13, 1980, Ashanti became the first female artist to hold the top two positions on the U.S. Billboard Hot 100 chart simultaneously, with “Foolish” and “What’s Luv?” claiming the first and second spots, respectively. In 2002, Ashanti launched her self-titled debut album, achieving impressive sales exceeding 505,000 copies in its first week in the U.S.

Is Ashanti Expecting First Child With Nelly?

On January 1, 2003, Ashanti crossed paths with the renowned rapper Nelly at a press conference for the 2003 Grammy Awards. Their encounter blossomed into a romantic journey, and the two embarked on a relationship that spanned a decade. However, life’s twists and turns led them on separate paths, and they decided to part ways after their decade-long companionship.

Yet, love, resilient and unpredictable, found a way to rewrite their story. In a surprising turn of events, Ashanti and Nelly rekindled their connection in the year 2023, reigniting the flame that had once burned brightly between them. The reunion brought a renewed sense of joy and companionship, marking a significant chapter in their shared history.

As the pages turned towards December 2023, another delightful revelation emerged – the couple was reportedly expecting their first child together. The news added a joyous and heartwarming dimension to their story, symbolizing a new beginning and the promise of family bliss. Fans and well-wishers eagerly anticipated the arrival of this newest chapter in the lives of Ashanti and Nelly, celebrating the love that had endured, evolved, and now blossomed into the anticipation of parenthood.

In 2003, she continued her success with the release of her second album, “Chapter II,” which debuted at number one on the Billboard 200. The album’s singles, “Rock wit U (Awww Baby)” and “Rain on Me,” achieved commercial success, reaching numbers two and seven, respectively, on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. “Chapter II” received a Grammy nomination for Best Contemporary R&B Album, while its singles earned nominations in the categories of Best R&B Song and Best Female R&B Vocal Performance.

Additionally, she unveiled a Christmas album, “Ashanti’s Christmas,” in November 2003, which achieved modest commercial success. In 2004, Ashanti unveiled her third studio album, “Concrete Rose,” paying homage to Tupac Shakur’s pseudonym, “The Rose That Grew from Concrete.” The album debuted at number seven in the U.S. with first-week sales of 254,000 copies, marking her third platinum-certified album.

Notably, the single “Only U” reached number thirteen on the Billboard Hot 100 and became her most significant hit in the United Kingdom, peaking at number two. Subsequent albums include “The Declaration” (2008) and “Braveheart” (2014), the latter released independently on Ashanti’s record label, Written Entertainment. Beyond her music career, Ashanti has ventured into acting.

In 2005, she made her feature film debut in “Coach Carter” alongside Samuel L. Jackson and later starred as Dorothy Gale in the made-for-television film “The Muppets’ Wizard of Oz.” Her acting repertoire extends to films such as “John Tucker Must Die” (2006) and “Resident Evil: Extinction” (2007). Throughout her illustrious career, Ashanti has sold nearly 15 million records worldwide, solidifying her status as a multifaceted and accomplished artist.
